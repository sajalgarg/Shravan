Admission Procedure

Mentally Challenged Children of mild to moderate level having IQ between 36 to 70 belonging to any religion caste, socio-economic status and language are eligible for admission.

First of all a child is assessed in the Diagnostic Clinic.

Group assessment is carried out for two weeks. While group assessment goes on one side, medical assessment & physio occupational assessment goes on the other side. All the assessment reports are collected and a conclusion is drawn. The parents of the children are guided/counselled accordingly. If a child is found eligible, he is granted admission. If seats are not vacant he/she is kept in the waiting list. The children who are not found eligible and parents of the child are willing to opt for the Home Training Programme are given Home Training. The children who are not found eligible are guided to other Institutions for admission.

Note:- If it is found that Parents are not sincere in implementing the Home Training Programme, the vacant seat available is allotted to other children in the waiting list.

Age Limit:

Residential Setting:

Minimum 3 years and maximum 10 years

Day-Care Setting

Minimum 3 years and maximum 18 years

Other Conditions for Admission under Residential Setting:-

The Child must be toilet trained
The Child must not be having epileptic seizures.
The Child must not be having Hyperkinesis.

Note

The Parents of Children (having hyperkinesis and not toilet trained) are helped for development of the child under Home Training Programme.
Preference is given in admission to those children who are on roll for Home training Programme and have shown satisfactory results.
Children with Borderline Mental Retardation are also given admission.

Courses Offered

Human Resource Development Training Programmes being run under regular and distance mode programmes:-

I Regular Mode Courses

a) Diploma in Education (Special Education)( Mental Retardation ) (DED) (SE) MR)

A two year regular course being run since 1993 provides trained manpower to work as Special Educators / Teachers to work in Special School ,Inclusive set up schools. The essential qualification is 10+2 pass. Admission is done on merit basis after qualifying All India On line Aptitude Test being conducted by the Rehabilitation Council of India, New Delhi. Total seat are 25. So far there is hundred percent placement . The course is recognized by the Rehabilitation Council of India, New Delhi.

b) Diploma in Early Childhood Special Education (DECSE)(MR)

A one year regular mode course, being run since 2010, provides trained manpower to work as special teacher specially for children up to pre-school level. The essential qualification is 10+2 pass. Admission is done on merit basis based upon percentage of marks obtained in 10+2 class. Total seat are 25. The course is recognized by the Rehabilitation Council of India , New Delhi.

c) Certificate Course in Care-Giving

A six month regular course being run since 2009provides trained manpower to work as Care Giver to provide services to children with developmental disabilities at their home and day- care centers. The essential qualification for admission is tenth pass. Admission is done on merit basis based upon percentage of marks obtained in tenth class. Total seat are 25. The course is recognized by the Rehabilitation Council of India , New Delhi.

2. Distance Mode Courses

a) BEd Special Education (Mental Retardation) (BEd)(SE)(DE)(MR)

A two year distance education course being run since 2000 provides trained manpower to work as professional in special school, inclusive set ups , Institution etc. The essential qualification is graduation. Admission is done by the Indira Gandhi National Open University (IGNOU) after conducting an on-line admission test at all – India Level . Total seat are 40. The course is recognized by the Rehabilitation Council of India and affiliated to IGNOU as a study centre.

b) Post Graduate Professional Diploma in Special Education (Mental Retardation) (PGPDSE):-(MR).

A one year distance education course being run since 2000 provides trained manpower to work as professional in Special Education. The essential qualification is BEd (General stream ). The student, after completing this course is treated as equitant to having complete BEd. SE. Admission is done by the Indira Gandhi National open University (IGNOU) on merit basis. Total seat are 40. The course is recognized by the Rehabilitation Council of India and affiliated to IGNOU as a study centre.

c) Post Graduate Professional Certificate Course in Special Education (PGPCSE)(MR)

A six month distance education course, being run since 2010, provides trained manpower to work as professional in more than one stream of disability. The essential qualification is BEd SE. Admission is done by Indira Gandhi National Open University on merit basis. Total seat are 40. The course is recognized by the Rehabilitation council of India and affiliate d to IGNOU as a study Centre.

d) Foundation Course on Special Education (FCSE)

A Three month distance education course, being run since 2005, provides orientation to trained teachers working in the mainstreamed school to work with children with special needs in their class. Admission is done by the Indira Gandhi National Open University. Total seat are 40. The Course is recognized by the Retaliation Council of India and affiliated to IGNOU as a study centre.

Bit more knowledge...

From the 1960s and 1970s, the process of de-institutionalization meant that many more individuals with mental health problems were able to live in their communities rather than being confined to mental institutions. Medication and psychotherapy were the two major treatment approaches, with little attention given to supporting and facilitating daily functioning and social interaction. Therapeutic interventions often had little impact on daily living, socialization and work opportunities. There were often barriers to social inclusion in the form of stigma and prejudice. Psychiatric rehabilitation work emerged with the aim of helping the community integration and independence of individuals with mental health problems. "Psychiatric rehabilitation" and "psychosocial rehabilitation" became used interchangeably, as terms for the same practice.These approaches may merge with or conflict with approaches based in the Psychiatric survivors movement, including the concept of user-controlled personal assistance services. Although current literature in the United States uses the names psychosocial rehabilitation and psychiatric rehabilitation interchangeably, around 2005 the professional organization IAPSRS (International Association of Psychosocial Rehabilitation Services) changed its name to USPRA (United States Psychiatric Rehabilitation Association) and the trend is toward the use of "psychiatric rehabilitation." In 2012, Temple University is funded in the field of psychiatric disabilities at a national center with the National Institute on Disability Rehabilitation Research (NIDRR), US Department of Education, having this population group as a priority.
